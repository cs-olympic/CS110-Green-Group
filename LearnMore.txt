A "weapon of math destruction" (WMD) is:
A large-scale algorithm or predictive model
That is opaque (hard to understand or challenge)
And causes unfair or damaging outcomes
While affecting lots of people

The term was coined by Cathy O'neil in her 2016 book by the same name. O'Neil is an mathematician
and data scientist. She earned a PhD in mathematics, worked in academia, then moved into finance as 
a quantitative analyst, and later became a data science consultant and public advocate
for algorithmic accountability.

Examples from the book

Credit scoring
Hiring and resume screening
Predictive policing
College rankings
Teacher evaluations
can reinforce inequality if they're biased or poorly designed.
